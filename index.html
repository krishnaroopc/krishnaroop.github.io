Research

My work sits at the intersection of polymer physics, soft matter mechanics, and experimental rheology — connecting molecular-scale dynamics to macroscopic material behavior.

01

Photoelastic Particle Fabrication

Fabricating mercury-free photoelastic polymer particles with optimal mechanical and optical properties for granular matter and suspension studies.

CU Boulder · Lab of Prof. Nathalie Vriend

02

Soft Wetting Mechanics

Measuring time-dependent wetting ridge growth on polymer melts using AFM and optical profilometry, and linking bulk rheology to surface dynamics.

University of Kentucky / Cincinnati · Lab of Prof. Jonathan Pham

03

Block Copolymer Coatings

Investigating small molecule contamination dynamics in soft block copolymer coatings using real-time confocal microscopy and MATLAB kinetics modeling.

University of Cincinnati · Lab of Prof. Jonathan Pham

04

Polyethylene Blend Rheology

Developing novel melt compounding techniques for UHMWPE/HDPE blends and a mathematical framework based on polymer chain entanglement dynamics.

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory · Lab of Prof. Ashish K. Lele

05

Rheological Data Digitization

Developing OpenRheo — a software tool that computationally identifies and extracts raw rheological data from historical PDF plots using image processing pipelines and ML.

Ongoing independent project
