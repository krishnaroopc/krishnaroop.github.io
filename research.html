Work

Research

My work connects molecular polymer dynamics to macroscopic material behavior, spanning granular systems, soft wetting, and polymer blends.

Figure

Photoelastic Particle Fabrication

Fabricating mercury-free photoelastic polymer particles with optimal mechanical and optical properties for granular matter and suspension studies. Characterizing suspensions using shear rheology, DMA, DSC, and optical microscopy.

CU Boulder · Lab of Prof. Nathalie Vriend · 2024–present

Figure

Soft Wetting Mechanics

Measuring time-dependent wetting ridge height on polymer melt surfaces using AFM and optical profilometry. Derived a mathematical relationship between temperature-dependent bulk rheology and surface growth rate of the wetting ridge.

University of Kentucky · Lab of Prof. Jonathan Pham · 2020–2023

Figure

Block Copolymer Coatings

Investigating small molecule contamination dynamics in soft block copolymer coatings using real-time confocal microscopy visualization. Formulated a MATLAB kinetics model to define dye contamination mechanisms.

University of Cincinnati · Lab of Prof. Jonathan Pham · 2023–2024

Figure

Polyethylene Blend Rheology

Designed a novel melt compounding technique for UHMWPE/HDPE blends. Developed a mathematical framework based on polymer chain entanglement dynamics to measure dissolution extent. Conducted extensional and oscillatory rheology to define optimal processing conditions.

CSIR-National Chemical Laboratory · Lab of Prof. Ashish K. Lele · 2014–2020

Figure

OpenRheo — Rheological Data Digitization

Developing a proprietary software tool that computationally identifies and extracts raw rheological data arrays from historical PDF plots. Built on image processing pipelines, physics-informed neural networks (PINNs), and MATLAB programming.

Independent project · Ongoing

Figure

Tribological Force Measurement

Built experimental instrumentation for tribological force measurements using LabVIEW for data acquisition. Trained graduate students in rheometry, atomic force microscopy (AFM), and optical profilometry.

University of Cincinnati · Lab of Prof. Jonathan Pham · 2023–2024
